Pain rule (non-negotiable): muscle burn or next-day soreness is fine. Sharp pain, pinching, or any pain inside a joint (knee, shoulder, wrist, lower back) — stop that exercise, use the easier version listed, and if it repeats twice, drop it and tell me so we can swap it. This plan is guidance, not medical advice; check with your doctor if any joint issue persists.
Progression rule (how muscle actually gets built): the week ranges on each exercise are guides, not commands. The real rule is earn it — when you hit the top of the rep range (or time) with clean form and quiet joints in two sessions in a row, move up: harder version, firmer band, or slower lowering. If you can't, stay where you are — that is the plan working, not failing.

Phase 1 · Foundation (Weeks 1–4)

Learn the patterns · effort ~5–6/10
Every rep slow and controlled. Use the easiest listed version of each exercise (wall push-ups, chair-assisted squats, knee planks). Stretches held 20–30s, never forced. The win here is showing up 4×/week and finishing pain-free.

Phase 2 · Build (Weeks 5–8)

Add range & light resistance · effort ~6–7/10
Move to the middle progressions (incline push-ups, band rows with a firmer band, full sit-to-stands without hands). Stretch holds go to 30–45s. Week 8 is a deload: halve all sets, keep the daily mobility — joints and tendons at 48 adapt slower than muscles and need this week.

Phase 3 · Express (Weeks 9–12)

Density & earned progressions · effort ~7/10
Shorter rests (45s), top-end progressions only if joints are quiet. Stretch holds 45–60s. Week 12 is a deload: halve all sets, keep the stretches, and re-test all five metrics in Track — this is your mid-program checkpoint.

Phase 4 · Consolidate (Weeks 13–16)

Where the visible change lands · effort ~7/10
Same movements, firmer bands, slower lowering (3s on everything), rests stay at 45–60s. By now the joints have had 12 weeks to adapt — these four weeks are where the chest, lats and abs you built actually become visible. Week 16: light week + final re-test of all five metrics. Then we design the next block together.
What changed from v1: the handstand goal is parked — repeated wrist loading is the highest joint-risk item in the old plan. Its slot now goes to posture (wall angels, chin tucks) and shoulder health. We can bring wall-supported holds back after week 12 if your wrists and shoulders feel strong.
What changed in v3: a lat pulldown was added on Tuesday (rows alone don’t widen lats), a second chest press on Thursday (one pressing move a week was too little for a built chest), progressions are now earned by reps instead of the calendar, a daily 7–8k step floor drives the waist goal, and the program extends to 16 weeks — weeks 13–16 are where the muscle you built becomes visible.
Handstand — gated goal: parked as an active move for joint safety, but re-introduced in stages. From now: wrist prep (see Tuesday) builds the tendons. From week 8: wall-supported holds, only if wrists and shoulders are quiet. The pain rule overrides everything — any wrist pinch and it regresses.
Leg split — flexibility goal: not a separate exercise but the target the Saturday hip/hamstring work (90/90, low lunge, lying hamstring, hip flexor) builds toward. Progress shows up as your sit-and-reach and hip range improving. Never force the bottom of a split — let range come from the held stretches over weeks.

Simple rules that do 90% of the work

  • Protein anchor: every meal has eggs, chicken, fish or thick curd. Roughly: 3 eggs ≈ 18g, 150g chicken ≈ 35–40g, 150g fish ≈ 28–32g, 1 cup curd ≈ 8g.
  • Rice discipline, not rice removal: one loose fist of cooked rice per meal; swap to ragi/millet dosa or chapati on alternate days. Vegetables fill the rest of the plate.
  • Frying: deep-fried items (chicken 65, bajji, vada) max once a week. Tawa, grill, curry and steam are the defaults.
  • Water: 2.5–3 litres/day. Buttermilk counts, sweet tea/coffee doesn't — cap sugar at 2 tsp/day total.
  • Recovery foods: curd (protein + gut), keerai/greens 3–4×/week (magnesium for muscles), turmeric + pepper in cooking as usual, fish 2×/week (omega-3 helps joints).
  • Optional shortcut: if protein is falling short, one scoop of whey in milk as the snack closes the gap cleanly.

Recovery is a scheduled part of the plan

  • Built-in spacing: no muscle group is trained hard on consecutive days. Wednesday and Sunday are protected recovery days — a 20–30 min easy walk is the only "training".
  • Deload weeks (8 & 12) + light week 16: halve every set count, keep all stretches. Tendons and joints at 48 recover on a 7–10 day cycle, not 48 hours — deloads are where they actually adapt.
  • Daily step floor — 7,000–8,000: spread through the day, not one forced march. This is the single biggest lever on the waist number; the sessions build the muscle, the steps burn the cover off it.
  • Cooldown breathing (every session): last 3 minutes — lie down, inhale 4 counts through the nose, exhale 6–8 counts. Long exhales switch the nervous system to recovery mode and measurably lower next-day stiffness.
  • Legs-up-the-wall (10 min): evenings after Monday and Friday sessions. Lie on your back, legs resting up a wall. Drains the legs, decompresses the lower back. Feels like nothing, works like magic.
  • Warm water: a warm bath or hot-water bag on the lower back on rest days if stiff. Heat before stretching, never force a cold muscle.
  • Sleep: 7–8 hours is when muscle is actually built. If sleep was under 6 hours, do only the warm-up + stretches that day and skip the strength block — that's a smart day, not a lost day.
  • Weekly joint check (Sunday): rate knees, lower back, shoulders, wrists out of 10. Anything at 3+ discomfort → next week use the easier progression for exercises touching that joint.
Soreness guide: dull muscle soreness 24–48h after a session = normal adaptation. Soreness lasting 3+ days = last session was too much; reduce one set from everything next time. Joint pain of any kind = never normal; regress immediately.
